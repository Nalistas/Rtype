Hides from ARIA everything, except provided node(s).

Helps to isolate modal dialogs and focused task -\/ the content will be not accessible using accessible tools.

Now with \href{https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/HTMLElement/inert}{\texttt{ HTML inert}} support\hypertarget{md__2home_2solype_2delivery_2current__days_2Mannheim_2front_2node__modules_2aria-hidden_2README_autotoc_md1342}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{API}{API}}\label{md__2home_2solype_2delivery_2current__days_2Mannheim_2front_2node__modules_2aria-hidden_2README_autotoc_md1342}
Just call {\ttfamily hide\+Others} with DOM-\/node you want to keep, and it will {\itshape hide} everything else. {\ttfamily target\+Node} could be placed anywhere -\/ its siblings would be hidden, but it and its parents -\/ not.

While {\ttfamily aria-\/hidden} played important role in the past and will play in the future -\/ the main use case always was around isolating content and making elements "{}transparent"{} not only for aria, but for user interaction as well.

This is why you might consider using {\ttfamily inert\+Others}
